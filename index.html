ISE 2023

We are thrilled to invite you to join us for the upcoming International Society of Endocytobiology (ISE) conference, to be held at the Field Museum in Chicago. This international event will bring together experts in the field of endocytobiology to share their latest research findings, exchange ideas, and build collaborations. The conference will feature a wide range of exciting topics, including the latest advances in endosymbiosis, cellular evolution, and symbiosis in eukaryotic cells. With a diverse lineup of world-renowned speakers, there will be plenty of opportunities to learn and engage with other professionals in the field.

We hope you will join us for this exciting event and contribute to the continued growth and advancement of endocytobiology research. Register will open soon - take advantage of early bird pricing.

Venue

The Field Museum

The Field Museum in Chicago is a world-renowned natural history museum that boasts an impressive collection of over 40 million specimens and artifacts. The museum's exhibits cover a wide range of topics, including biology, geology, anthropology, and archaeology, showcasing the diversity of life on Earth and the fascinating cultures of different civilizations throughout history. Visitors can explore the museum's extensive collection of dinosaur fossils, including the famous T-Rex named Sue, as well as learn about the different ecosystems and environments that exist on our planet. With its engaging and informative displays, the Field Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the natural world and human history.

Organizer

International Society of Endocytobiology (ISE)

The International Society of Endocytobiology (ISE) is a professional organization that focuses on the study of endosymbiosis and symbiosis. It brings together researchers and scholars from different fields to promote collaboration and advance the understanding of this biological phenomenon. ISE hosts conferences and workshops, publishes scientific journals, and supports student research through awards and grants.
